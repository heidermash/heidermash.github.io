01 / About me

Research that moves from evidence to action.

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computational Science and Engineering at McMaster University and Research Coordinator at the Smart Freight Centre.

My work sits at the intersection of operations research, data analytics, sustainable urban freight, and supply-chain decision support. I combine survey evidence, administrative and open data, mathematical optimization, and computational experiments to design service systems that are analytically rigorous and useful in practice.

04 / Optimization research

Behavior, networks, and low-emission freight.

Active research is described by scope rather than unpublished manuscript titles, target journals, or submission dates.

01

Behaviorally informed parcel-locker location

Embedding willingness-to-travel evidence from 2,617 Canadian respondents into capacitated facility-location decisions.

02

Contiguous zero-emission delivery zones

Designing connected urban zones with endogenous district counts through decomposition and connectivity cuts.

03

Strategic fleet planning under access restrictions

Coordinating acquisition, retrofit, charging, scrappage, and delivery assignment under emissions policy.

04

Urban-freight evidence and policy screening

Connecting a curated evidence base to bounded Toronto neighbourhood screening without overstating demand, emissions, or public support.
